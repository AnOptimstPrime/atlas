Coach Marsh's notes on your history

Left Knee
2018 ACL/MCL/partial meniscus tear, patellar tendon graft. Graft is intact and healthy. A new tear in the lateral meniscus and a lesion on the lateral femoral condyle mean the lateral side of the joint is the area to load carefully right now.
  • Build up deep knee flexion under load gradually — don't jump straight to heavy full-depth squats or lunges.
  • Single-leg landing and deceleration work is the priority, not something to avoid — that's how the confidence (and the stability) comes back.
  • Low-impact conditioning (bike, rower, sled) is a smart substitute for high-impact cardio on heavy training days.
  • Any new swelling, locking, or sharp catching pain is a stop-and-check signal, not a push-through signal.
Right Shoulder
Moderate supraspinatus tendinopathy with a small partial tear, mild bursitis, and mild AC joint irritation. A possible labral (SLAP) finding is still being followed up on. The rotator cuff is otherwise intact.
  • Cuff and scapular stability work (face pulls, band pull-aparts, external rotation) earns a permanent spot in the program, not just a warm-up afterthought.
  • Heavy overhead pressing and behind-the-neck movements are the ones to be most conservative with.
  • Favor angles that keep the shoulder in a comfortable arc — landmine press, incline press, neutral-grip work — over end-range overhead loading.
  • Sharp or catching pain, not just normal training fatigue, means back off and get it looked at.
